# Writing Sample (MBA Dissertation)
(Revised excerpt submitted as a writing sample to demonstrate qualitative analysis and research discipline)

## Title

Assessing the Impact of FinTech Disruptive Capabilities on High Street Banks in the United Kingdom

## 1. Context and Research Question

This study examines how FinTech-driven disruptive capabilities have reshaped competitive dynamics, operational practices, and strategic responses among high street banks in the United Kingdom. The purpose of this writing sample is to demonstrate analytical clarity, methodological discipline, and evidence-based reasoning using secondary qualitative data.

The UK retail banking sector provides an appropriate empirical context due to its mature regulatory environment, dense branch infrastructure, and early exposure to FinTech challengers.

Research question: How do FinTech disruptive capabilities reshape organisational cost structures, service delivery practices, and strategic responses within incumbent UK high street banks?

## 2. Method

The study adopts a qualitative-dominant research design based on secondary data analysis. Data sources include industry reports, regulatory publications, policy documents, and peer-reviewed literature relevant to UK retail banking.

A purposive sampling strategy prioritised sources addressing FinTech-enabled capabilities such as digital payments, mobile banking platforms, open banking infrastructure, and automation. Sources were selected for analytical relevance rather than representativeness, reflecting an interpretive aim of identifying mechanisms and patterns rather than estimating causal effects.

Analysis followed a thematic synthesis approach. Texts were coded iteratively, with initial descriptive codes refined into higher-order analytic themes through comparison across sources.

Methodological limitations were acknowledged at the outset. Reliance on secondary data restricts access to internal decision-making processes and limits causal inference. Findings should therefore be interpreted as analytic insights rather than predictive claims.

## 3. Findings and Analysis

### 3.1 Organisational Cost Reconfiguration

FinTech capabilities exert sustained pressure on incumbent cost structures by reducing marginal transaction costs and enabling automation of routine banking processes. Digital-first challengers operate with leaner infrastructure, exposing inefficiencies embedded in branch-based operating models.

Responses among high street banks were uneven. Some pursued incremental digital upgrades layered onto existing systems, while others initiated broader platform modernisation programmes. These divergent responses reflect differences in risk tolerance, capital availability, and organisational readiness.

### 3.2 Reconfiguration of Service Practices and Expectations

FinTech platforms foreground immediacy, transparency, and self-service, reshaping customer expectations of banking service. User experience design becomes a competitive differentiator rather than a peripheral concern.

Incumbent banks face constraints in replicating these experiences due to legacy systems and regulatory complexity. As a result, experience innovation often lags behind technological capability.

### 3.3 Heterogeneity in Organisational Responses

Strategic responses among incumbent banks vary widely, ranging from partnerships and acquisitions to internally developed innovation units. These strategies reflect underlying governance structures, organisational culture, and historical investment decisions.

Rather than convergence, the sector exhibits increasing heterogeneity in strategic positioning relative to FinTech entrants.

## 4. Conclusion and Limits

This study demonstrates the ability to frame a researchable question, justify methodological choices, and derive analytic claims from qualitative evidence. FinTech disruption operates through multiple interacting mechanisms affecting cost structures, customer experience, and strategic orientation.

Claims are limited to the UK retail banking context and to interpretive insights drawn from secondary data. The primary value of this work lies in methodological training in structured qualitative analysis, critical synthesis, and disciplined argumentation.

As a PhD writing sample, this excerpt is provided to evidence qualitative research discipline, transparent scoping of claims, and careful handling of interpretive limits.
